About Me

I am a 33-year-old female with a Bachelor's Degree in Mathematics and a Minor in Computer Science. Since 2021, I have been using sites such as W3Schools and Codecademy to deepen and expand my programming knowledge. In 2016, I began working as a full-time high school math teacher, but I have grown tired of the low pay coupled with the long hours. Programming has always interested me, and I have always loved technology, puzzles, and challenging my mind. In the past, I have done some minor modding of computer games and created complex excel formulas and conditional formatting at work to make my job easier.

I greatly enjoyed learning new programming languages and how to build and modify websites. In general, I like working with the logic of Python, Java, and Javascript over the more open-ended languages such as HTML or CSS. My goal is to become a remote Software Engineer, but I am currently hoping to find a remote job as a Junior Developer to gain the necessary experience to be successful. I prefer to work with Javascript as a back-end developer, but I am comfortable with the front-end as well. As someone who has used multiple websites and programs that are less than ideal and felt the frustration that comes from an incomplete design, I am passionate about helping to create websites that provide an optimal and seamless user experience.

Find Your Hat

Languages used: Javascript, Node,

An interactive terminal game from a Codecademy assignment. The scenario is that the player has lost their hat in a field full of holes, and they must navigate back to it without falling down one of the holes or stepping outside of the field. This project required a TON of troubleshooting to figure out the proper logic. I am quite proud of the final result.

I created 2 versions of this game:

  • One based purely in Javascript that I hope to eventually create a github page with
  • And one on Replit.com that focuses on the use of the terminal kit and the customization that provides
